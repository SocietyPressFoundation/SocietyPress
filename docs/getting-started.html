Getting Started

Congratulations on installing SocietyPress! This guide walks you through the essential first steps to get your membership site up and running.

Logging Into WordPress Admin

To manage your SocietyPress site, you'll use the WordPress admin dashboard.

  1. Go to your admin login page
    Visit: https://yourwebsite.com/wp-admin
    (Replace "yourwebsite.com" with your actual domain)
  2. Enter your credentials
    Use the username and password you created during installation. If you used the installer, these were displayed on the final screen.
  3. You're in!
    You'll see the WordPress Dashboard with the SocietyPress menu on the left.
Bookmark Your Admin Page

Save yourwebsite.com/wp-admin as a bookmark in your browser so you can quickly access it later.

Understanding the SocietyPress Menu

In the WordPress admin sidebar, you'll find the SocietyPress menu. Here's what each section does:

Menu Item What It Does
Dashboard Overview of your society: member counts, recent activity, expiring memberships
Leadership Manage board positions, officer roles, and who holds them
Committees Create committees and assign members with roles (Chair, Member, etc.)
Calendar View and manage all your society's events
Add New Event Create a new class, workshop, meeting, or other event
Members View, search, filter, and manage all your members
Add New Member Manually add a new member to your roster
Import Members Import members from a spreadsheet (CSV, Excel)
Member Levels Configure membership tiers (Individual, Family, Lifetime, etc.)
Settings Configure organization info, email settings, and other options

Step 1: Configure Your Organization

The first thing you should do is enter your organization's information. This information appears throughout your site and in member communications.

  1. Go to SocietyPress → Settings
    Click "Settings" in the SocietyPress menu.
  2. Fill in the Organization section
    Enter your society's:
    • Name (e.g., "Springfield Genealogical Society")
    • Street address
    • Phone number
    • Email address
  3. Save your changes
    Scroll down and click the "Save Changes" button.

Step 2: Review Membership Tiers

SocietyPress comes with five default membership levels. You should review these and adjust them for your organization.

  1. Go to SocietyPress → Member Levels
  2. Review the default tiers
    The defaults are:
    • Individual ($35/year)
    • Family ($45/year)
    • Student ($20/year)
    • Lifetime ($500 one-time)
    • Institutional ($50/year)
  3. Edit as needed
    Click on any tier to change its name, price, or duration. You can also add new tiers or delete ones you don't need.
About Pricing

The prices shown are examples. Change them to match your organization's actual dues structure. SocietyPress doesn't process payments automatically (yet) — it just tracks membership status.

Step 3: Add Your First Member

Let's add a test member to see how the system works.

  1. Go to SocietyPress → Add New Member
  2. Fill in the basic information
    Enter at least:
    • First name and last name
    • Email address
    • Membership tier (select from the dropdown)
    • Status (Active, Pending, etc.)
  3. Add genealogy information (optional)
    Scroll down to add:
    • Surnames they're researching
    • Geographic areas of interest
  4. Click "Add Member"
    The member is now in your system!

Go to SocietyPress → Members to see your member list. You can search, filter, and sort members from this screen.

Step 4: Create Your First Event

Events are a core feature of SocietyPress. Let's create a sample event.

  1. Go to SocietyPress → Add New Event
  2. Enter the event details
    • Title (e.g., "Beginning Genealogy Workshop")
    • Description (use the editor to format text)
    • Date and time
    • Location
  3. Set registration options
    If the event requires registration, check the box and optionally set up time slots with capacity limits.
  4. Publish the event
    Click the "Publish" button in the top right.

Your event now appears on your site's calendar and can be viewed by visitors. Members can register for events if registration is enabled.

Step 5: Customize Your Theme

The SocietyPress theme can be customized to match your organization's branding.

  1. Go to Appearance → Customize
    This opens the WordPress Customizer with a live preview of your site.
  2. Add your logo
    Click "Site Identity" and upload your organization's logo.
  3. Adjust colors
    Click "Colors" to change the primary color, header color, and other elements to match your branding.
  4. Publish your changes
    When you're happy with how things look, click "Publish" at the top.

Step 6: Set Up Navigation Menus

Your site needs menus to help visitors navigate. SocietyPress theme supports three menu locations.

  1. Go to Appearance → Menus
  2. Create a new menu
    Enter a name like "Main Navigation" and click "Create Menu."
  3. Add pages to the menu
    Check the pages you want in the menu and click "Add to Menu." Drag items to reorder them.
  4. Assign the menu to a location
    Under "Menu Settings," check "Primary Menu" to make this your main navigation.
  5. Save the menu
    Click "Save Menu."
Smart Menu Filtering

The SocietyPress theme automatically hides certain menu items based on login status. For example, "Join" links hide for logged-in members, and "Member Directory" hides for visitors who aren't logged in.
